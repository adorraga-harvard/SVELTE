From Identity to Empowerment

A Policy Critique of the Philippine National ID System and a Blueprint for Reform

Amador Raga, July 2025

The Philippine Identification System (PhilSys) offers a centralized identity framework, but its current top-down structure raises critical issues of accessibility, security, adaptability, and citizen empowerment. This white paper critiques the prevailing model and proposes a federated, bottom-up alternative: Pinoy.ID.

1. The Current Approach: PhilSys

1.1 Overview

  • Lead Implementor: Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)
  • Outputs: Physical PhilID, ePhilID, digital credentials
  • Platform: Central biometric + demographic database

1.2 Structural Shortcomings

2. An Alternative Model: Pinoy.ID

2.1 Principles of Reform

  • Infrastructure by DICT, validation by LGUs
  • Multi-path registration (digital or manual)
  • Citizen-centric, modular, API-ready

4. Ethical Design: Authentication ‚â† Identity

PhilID conflates identification, authentication, and access entitlement. With biometric imprint, QR-linked database lookups, and mandatory presentation, the card enables profiling and overreach.

ConcernImpact
Biometric OverexposureCannot be revoked if compromised
Service CouplingSingle UID linked to all transactions
Profile CollusionSurveillance risk without oversight

Pinoy.ID proposes:

  • Simple cards with UID only
  • Biometric auth for high-risk use only
  • Consent and audit logs for every identity lookup

5. Policy Recommendations

  1. Transition PSA into a consumer of identity data, not a sole implementor
  2. Establish LGU validation as a civic right
  3. Deploy identity APIs via DICT for composable services
  4. Enshrine citizen consent, opt-out, and data visibility in law
