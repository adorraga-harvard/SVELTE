PDEA Executive Information System Database Documentation

I. Lookup Tables

These tables contain reference data, providing specific, static options for other tables. They are all identified by the lookup_ prefix.

  • lookup_barrios: Stores geographical information including barrio, city, province, and region names and codes.
  • lookup_biometric_types: Lists different types of biometrics with a name and description.
  • lookup_cases_status: Defines the possible statuses for a case, with a name and description.
  • lookup_drugs_schedule: Stores drug schedules with a name and description.
  • lookup_drugs_type: Lists the types of drugs.
  • lookup_drugs_vendor_category: Classifies categories of drug vendors.
  • lookup_drugs_vendor_status: Defines the status of a drug vendor.
  • lookup_drugs_vendor_type: Differentiates types of drug vendors.
  • lookup_fullnames: A reference table for full names and genders.
  • lookup_legal_courts: Stores a list of legal courts by name, city, and province.
  • lookup_legal_order_types: Defines the types of legal court orders.
  • lookup_operations_agency_partners: Lists partner agencies for operations with their acronyms and descriptions.
  • lookup_operations_approvers: Lists individuals authorized to approve operations, with their rank, position, and PDEA unit.
  • lookup_operations_roles: Defines the roles involved in operations.
  • lookup_operations_types: Lists the types of operations.
  • lookup_pdea_ranks: Defines the official ranks within PDEA.
  • lookup_pdea_units: Lists the different units within PDEA.
  • lookup_regions: Stores a list of regions.
  • lookup_syndicate_roles: Defines the roles within a syndicate.

II. Main Data Tables

These are the primary tables that hold the core operational data.

  • drugs_roster
  • drugs_vendors
  • cases_master
  • legal_court_orders
  • operations_coc
  • operations_master
  • pdea_personnel
  • poi_biometrics
  • poi_contacts
  • poi_roster
  • poi_syndicates

III. Bridge Tables

These tables are used to resolve many-to-many relationships between two or more main data tables.

  • drugs_vendors_map: Links drugs_vendors to drugs_roster.
  • cases_drugs: Links cases_master to drugs_roster, detailing the quantity and seizure location of drugs in a case.
  • cases_poi: Connects cases_master and poi_roster, defining a person's role in a case and their date of arrest.
  • poi_syndicate_members: Links poi_roster to poi_syndicates and lookup_syndicate_roles, showing which persons of interest belong to which syndicates and their specific role.
  • operations_master_operators: Links operations_master to pdea_personnel and lookup_operations_roles, detailing which personnel are assigned to an operation and their role.

IV. Hierarchy and Relationships

The hierarchy starts with the lookup_ tables which provide the foundational reference data. The main data tables contain the core records and reference the lookup tables via foreign keys. The bridge tables connect the main data tables to model complex relationships.

The relationships and their direction are as follows:

  • cases_drugs has a foreign key case_id pointing to cases_master and drug_id pointing to drugs_roster.
  • cases_master has a foreign key status_id pointing to lookup_cases_status and master_id pointing to operations_master.
  • cases_poi has case_id pointing to cases_master and poi_id pointing to poi_roster.
  • drugs_roster has foreign keys schedule_id pointing to lookup_drugs_schedule and type_id pointing to lookup_drugs_type.
  • drugs_vendors has foreign keys status_id pointing to lookup_drugs_vendor_status and vendor_type_id pointing to lookup_drugs_vendor_type.
  • drugs_vendors_map has a foreign key vendor_id pointing to drugs_vendors.
  • legal_court_orders has foreign keys case_id pointing to cases_master, issuing_court_id pointing to lookup_legal_courts, and order_type_id pointing to lookup_legal_order_types.
  • operations_coc has foreign keys approver_id pointing to lookup_operations_approvers and region_id pointing to lookup_regions.
  • operations_master has foreign keys operation_type_id pointing to lookup_operations_types, region_id pointing to lookup_regions, and coc_id pointing to operations_coc.
  • operations_master_operators has foreign keys role_id pointing to lookup_operations_roles, master_id pointing to operations_master, and personnel_id pointing to pdea_personnel.
  • pdea_personnel has foreign keys rank_id pointing to lookup_pdea_ranks and unit_id pointing to lookup_pdea_units.
  • poi_biometrics, poi_contacts, and cases_poi all have poi_id foreign keys pointing to poi_roster.
  • poi_syndicate_members has foreign keys role_id pointing to lookup_syndicate_roles, poi_id pointing to poi_roster, and syndicate_id pointing to poi_syndicates.
